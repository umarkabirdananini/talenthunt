Tech Talent Hunt Initiative

Tech Talent Hunt Initiative is a Non-Governmental Organization. It began operation on the 26th of March 2020 and its headquarters is situated in Sokoto State, Nigeria. It prioritizes four (4) member management team and Board of Directors – the Chairman, the Vice Chairman, the Secretary, the Treasurer and seven (7) other members.
The establishment of the organization was driven by the Management team's passion for humanitarian aid in education, mental health, digital technology, job and wealth creation as well as increasing number of idle and wasting talents, innovative ideas and creativities of most young IDPs in Nigeria. Direct beneficiaries of the organization's programs are IDPs who have at least secondary school education and good analytical skills, both males and females (Age: 15 - 24), irrespective of tribe and religion. 

ABOUT US
Tech Talent Hunt Initiative is mostly interested in the welfare of IDPs. We believe that being Internally Displaced Persons is a circumstance, not an identity – it does not define or limit what someone can become or achieve.
Collaborative approach, which takes into account the resources and expertise of established partners is enabling us to expand. And we have excellent track records of getting sponsors and hiring partners for the Trained IDP-Beneficiaries easier as well as having mentors who guide us on the right partnerships and ways for sustainability. Before establishing Tech Talent Hunt Initiative, we tapped into corporate volunteerism in Sokoto State Nigeria as a volunteer-support organization.

ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE
Tech Talent Hunt Initiative is a Non-Governmental Organization. It is incorporated with Corporate Affairs Commission of the Federal Republic of Nigeria with registration number: 184240 on the 13th of July 2022. 
With over sixty six (66) Tech Trainer Experts, fifty (50) Assistant Trainers, forty two (42) Soft Skills Trainer Experts, twenty five (25) Fundraising Facilitators and twenty (20) Partnership Facilitators, the organization has been able to offer multiple and impactful services for Internally Displaced Persons in North West Nigeria. The organization has a core staff of fifteen (15) and finances its programs through grants, donations, fund raising and personal contributions.

IMPACTS
Tech Talent Hunt Initiative has provided competitive skills and economic opportunities to hundreds of thousands of Internally Displaced Persons in North West Nigeria who are now self-sustained and earning competitive wages. They have addded to digital tech workforce and are spuring productivity in helping to build stronger and more regional stable economies. The organization is able to achieve these through dedicated efforts of the Tech Trainer Experts, Trainer Assistants, Soft Skills Trainers, Fundraising Facilitators, Partnership Facilitators, well-achieved Directors, compassionate Sponsors/Partners and ambitious Management Team.

OUR VISION 
Tech Talent Hunt Initiative envisages a society where the dreams of young and smart IDPs are achieved and a society where talents, creativities and innovative ideas that could be harnessed to elevate digital tech of Africa and global economy are nurtured, not wasted.

MISSION STATEMENT
Tech Talent Hunt Initiative is a Non-Governmental Organization that assists young IDPs to be self-reliant and progress beyond humanitarian assistance through provision of digital skills, employability skills, competitive digital jobs and contracts, in collaboration with hiring and infrastructure partners, community stakeholders, local and international NGOs.

IDPs IN NORTH WEST NIGERIA

Despite military operations, insecurity in North West Nigeria is still active and continue to rise.

Forceful displacement of households in North West Nigeria is as a result of a multidimensional crisis such as armed banditry, terrorism and alleged genocide attacks on vulnerable communities – one that is rooted in historic ethno-social cleavages that rekindled in 2013. As more disheartening as it could be, there has been rapid increase in the number of victims since the attacks intensified in 2018.

At the end of December 2021 alone, over 89,629 persons were displaced in Kaduna; 50,676 in Kano; 121,434 in Katsina; 45,402 in Sokoto; and 112,316 in Zamfara state. Currently, the number of displaced persons in the region is approaching 1,000,000. Among the displaced persons, 54% are females and 46% are males; 56% are children (0-17 years), 37% are adults (18-59 years) and 7% are elderly (60 years and above). 
Displaced persons now reside in IDP-Camps, Camp-Like Settings or among Host Community Settings because of the fear of being attack again on their home return. While 16% of them reside in IDP-camps and Camp-like settings, 84% reside among host community settings.
